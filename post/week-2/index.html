In twenty years there will be fewer cars in use than there are today.

Since the invention of cars, it has been playing a crucial role in our daily life. It’s hard to imagine a day without cars. In the last decades, the amount of cars is soaring impressively. Hence, some folks drew the conclusion that in the next twenty years, there will be more cars than today. But from my perspective, the number of cars will be reduced tremendously. There are mainly three factors to support my opinion.

First of all, considering the fast pace of technology development, different transportation means will emerge: high-speed trains, airplanes, maglevs, etc. As the technology becoming mature, these vehicles are no longer expensive for the majority of us. When we travel a long distance, taking a high-speed train is more economical, convenient and comfortable. When it comes to short distance, the subway system in twenty years will be integrated enough for citizens to transporting. Thus, it is not wise to choose cars as vehicles in the next twenty years.

Secondly, if we own a car, there are a lot of issues to consider about: the most important one is refueling, the oil price is rising all the time, which means there are fewer and fewer oil resources remain. As is universally acknowledged, oil is a non-renewable resource, which means once it is used up, it does not exist anymore. All of the cars using oil to fuel would become a bunch of irons. Another dilemma is the parking problem. In quite a few cities of China, it is hard to find a parking lot in the downtown area–since there are too many cars. Also, in commuting hours, it is common to be caught in a traffic jam. What’s more, the increasing vehicle fumes are harming the health of millions of citizens. However, it is glad to find many commuters have realized the drawbacks of cars and turned to bikes or scooters.

Finally, we have some good news: the emerge of the Internet has provided another option for traveling–the sharing cars. Aimed at coping with the car problem, the concept of sharing cars is to reduce the number of cars and cut down the pollution. These cars are electric-fueled, that is to say, no emission will be produced. We can find them everywhere in the city, once a person is certificated, he or she can drive the car. After the ride, you just need to park the car in a public area and pay for the ride. This system has already put into practice in several big cities in China. In Shanghai, according to an investigation, the traffic condition has become better since the using of sharing cars.

All in all, it is an inevitable trend that the number of cars will be reduced in the next twenty years. The life in the future can be more desirable with less car.
