Sunshine

2018-03-14 约 465 字 预计阅读 1 分钟 次阅读

Today is a cute day. Flowers blooming, birds singing.

It is more important for students to understand ideas and concepts than it is for them to learn facts.

As a famous saying goes: Practice is the sole criterion for testing truth. Like millions of the students in the world, we are dealing with books and papers every day, whenever we learn something new at class, we have to understand or even memorize the significant ideas and concepts of it. No matter for personal interests or simply for the exam.

It may seem a quick and straightforward way to absorb knowledge by learning the basic ideas and concepts, however, on the contrary, it took more time and achieve less efficiency. Take my favorite subject, biology, as an example, since ideas and concepts written in our textbooks come from thousands of experiments and observations of former scientists and researchers, it took them years to come to final conclusion. Nevertheless, in classes, we learn them in several sentences or equations. We, students, are often told that if this parameter is changed, what would happen, but we don’t really put this into practice, maybe after the exam, those equations and principles will be soon forgotten. Thus, it is not a good way for students’ development, on the opposite, it merely adds the difficulty to the curriculum and increases the burden on students.

As a matter of fact, there is one way that is less painful for students to learn something new–to learn the facts behind that knowledge, that is to say, to learn from the experiences, not the textbooks. As I see it, if more experimental classes were provided, students will definitely engage more time discovering the knowledge they are interested in, instead of memorizing boring concepts and theories. Consequently, they can catch up the knowledge faster while less painfully. In this way, they are putting the theorems and equations they learned into practice, not only their manipulative ability increased, but also the understanding of the contents gets deeper.

More and more schools started switching the teaching methods. In Xidian University, students are required to select eight different physical experiments each semester. In my perspective, this policy really helps students understand the theorems we learned in physics class. Anyway, seeing is believing, witnessing the fact speaks one thousand times louder than words.

All in all, facts are the base of our knowledge system while ideas and concepts are just the means to interpret the knowledge. There are various types of interpretation, but the facts remain the same forever. I would claim that learning facts is more important than understanding ideas and concepts in students’ studies.

写得慢，写得少。
