Playing computer games is a waste of time. Children should not be allowed to play them.

In 21th century, it is hard to find an individual who has never played computer games, let alone children who are more interested in those fantastic attractions. Since computer games’ first invention, millions of people are buying them, playing them, and watching them. For quiet a few people, computer game is an indispensable part of their childhood. The happiness and sense of completeness they bring to people directly illustrated the significance of computer games. Consequently, I would argue that children should feel free to play computer games and enjoy the ride.

As time changes, the way of entertaining changes, too. In the old days, our parents may chase after others in the yard or flying kites on the playground, nevertheless, in this period of time, children have countless computer games to choose: from shooting games to RPG, from MineCraft to car-racing, from dress-up to space exploration… The biggest advantage of playing computer games is that you can be whatever you wanted to be in your expectation, as to kids, who are on their way to explore their essential interest, playing different types of games definitely helps. One kid may come across Star Wars and get inspired by the mysterious outer space, and decided to learn more in the future. Or else in some strategy games which require teamwork, kids can learn to cooperate and increase the intelligence. Thus, computer games enabled them to experience different lives and lead them to find what they like, what’s more, also help them in the intellectual activities.

In London people love to talk about weather. But in schools of China, the most thing children are talking about is computer games. Since computers are basically widespread in China, the sad truth is, if you don’t play anything games, you are likely to be isolated from your peers. When you find your classmates are discussing the newest released on-line game but you have no idea what that is, that should be an embarrassing moment. It is hard to accept but it is even more hard to make a difference to this trend, as a kid. As far as I’m concerned, as a parent, it is better to allow children to play moderately rather than completely prohibit them from computer games.
